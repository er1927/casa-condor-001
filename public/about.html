About the project

This platform documents ecological restoration efforts for current and future generations, providing a source of help, hope, and companionship. It is not a how-to guide, a news outlet, or a debate space about the reality of climate change or the impact of patriarchy, capitalism, and monotheistic religions on our lands and bodies. Located in the sub-Antarctic forest on stolen land in Abya Yala, I experiment with regenerative forestry techniques to find what works best for the region amidst the evolving challenges of climate change. This work reflects my belief that despite the worsening climate crisis and the general state of the world, the survival of life on Earth depends on embracing our role as a keystone species and fostering symbiotic relationships within ecosystems.

About me

I am a multilingual, mixed-race woman, descended from colonizers, native peoples, and transcontinental slaves, born and raised on the periphery of the western empire. My livelihood comes from teaching, but my passions span writing, farming, walking, cooking, birdwatching, and lifelong learning.
Paraphrasing Nancy Fraser, I believe that the goal of human activity should be the production of life, not the accumulation of wealth. I work to promote life, and it is my heartfelt desire to encourage you to do the same.
